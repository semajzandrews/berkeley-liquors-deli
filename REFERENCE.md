# REFERENCE.md — Berkeley Liquors & Deli

External award-grade references only (Awwwards / FWA / Behance, last ~18 months).
No internal builds consulted. The bar is set by these, not by neighbors.

## The governing insight
Current award winners in the wine/spirits space REJECT the dark "premium liquor,
gold-foil, cellar-at-night" cliche. They go WARM, BRIGHT, INCLUSIVE. Berkeley opens
at 10 AM and runs a sandwich counter, so the honest world is DAYTIME corner store,
not nightfall bottle glow. This is also the cleanest divergence from both same-stack
neighbors, which are explicitly dark/night-themed.

## Bottle shop / wine references
- **Top Cuvée — https://www.shopcuvee.com/** — a wine shop that is ALSO a physical
  venue. The canonical dual-identity playbook: ONE voice, a forked navigation (Shop
  vs Visit), shared furniture. Tagline "Wine For Everyone!" rejects preciousness.
  Awwwards listing: https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/low-intervention-natural-wine-shop-subscription-1
- **Post Familiar Wine — https://www.awwwards.com/sites/post-familiar-wine-2** —
  SOTD. Technique stolen: typography-as-hero; bottles are punctuation, not wallpaper.
- **"Vine" — https://www.awwwards.com/sites/vine-website** — HM. Source of the warm
  ground idea (theirs: forest green + cream). We deliberately did NOT reuse green/
  cream (a neighbor owns green); we kept the warmth, swapped to butcher-paper + navy
  + awning red.

## Deli / counter references
- **Banh Mi World — https://banhmiworld.ca/** — HM. The "deli with character"
  recipe: bread-tone cream grounds, big deconstructed stacked display type, modular
  appetizing food cards on neutral grounds, first-person family voice.
- **La Revoltosa — https://larevoltosa.es/** — SOTD. Warm neighborhood-signage red,
  centered product photography, progressive-disclosure micro-interactions (fields/
  content revealed in paced steps). We adapt the pacing idea into the pour reveal.

## Type + palette direction taken
- Palette: **butcher-paper cream ground** (#F3EAD8), **navy ink** (#1A2F4A, the bottle
  lane, matches the wired BWYW accent), **awning red** (#C0392B, the counter lane),
  **mustard** (#D98E2B, price-tag pops). Light, daytime, grocer-warm, NOT dark.
- Type: **Clash Display** (Fontshare) for the stacked wordmark / heads (Banh Mi World
  deconstructed-type lineage), **General Sans** (Fontshare) body, **Space Mono**
  (Google) as the receipt/price-ticket label voice. None shared with neighbors
  (they use Melodrama/Supreme and Libre Caslon/Switzer).

## Signature derivation
Two craft verbs surfaced: POUR (wine into a glass) and STACK/WRAP (the sandwich).
We ship POUR as the single signature (clear verb, clear empty->full state change,
GPU-cheap clip-path fill, finger becomes the bottle). The deli identity is carried
structurally by a clearly art-directed second lane, not a competing heavy interaction.
See SIGNATURE.md.
